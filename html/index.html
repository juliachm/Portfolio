Transformative design

Redesign of Sensio Applications:
Transforming User Experiences

In one of my interaction design project at the university, as part of the Transformative Design course, I and two other students collaborated with Sensio, the market leader in welfare technology in Norway. The aim was to improve the user experience of their two apps, primarily focusing on two components: sustainability and democracy. We chose to focus on the elderly living at home, their relatives, and the assistance provided by volunteers.

Several interviews and workshops with various stakeholders (Sensio, the elderly, relatives, volunteers, the municipality, senior centers) helped us to understand the complexity of this wicked problem. Based on the data analysis, we developed a prototype of an app that combines aspects of two Sensio apps and includes collaboration with volunteers.

This project, called 'Design for Dignity,' was delivered to Sensio, who can further use it for the development of their solutions.

Web design / Development

Development of Dyktig AS website

Through my part-time job at Dyktig AS, I was tasked with creating a website for the company. The client wanted a clean and professional website focused on showcasing their employees and clients.

To gather inspiration for the design, I began by examining Dyktig's logo and Facebook profile. I extracted colors, fonts, and also looked at other consulting companies for inspiration. Then, I created simple sketches and discussed them with the client. After receiving feedback, I proceeded to develop a high-fidelity prototype using Figma.

I have recently finished developing the website using the React framework with the help of Vite and Styled components and I am excited to continue expanding this website in the future.
